# References

Reference list for *A Common Electrical-Energy Basis for Lunar Propellant
Production Routes*. Bibliographic details verified by web search and, where the
source is held in `raw/`, cross-checked against the document itself
(2026-05-30). Each entry notes what it supports in the model.

**Verification key:** entries are marked *[verified]* when authors, year, venue,
volume/pages or report ID, and DOI/URL were confirmed against a primary or
authoritative secondary source. *[unverified — best available]* marks entries
where one or more fields could not be confirmed to primary-source precision.

## Primary sources

1. Leger, D., Thoresen, F., Ghaffari-Tabrizi, F., Shaw, M., Rasera, J., Dickson,
   D., Valentin, B., Morlock, A., & Cowley, A. (2025). Modeling energy
   requirements for oxygen production on the Moon. *Proceedings of the National
   Academy of Sciences (PNAS)*, **122**(8), e2306146122.
   https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.2306146122
   *[verified — confirmed against raw/leger-2025-oxygen-production-energy-model-pnas.pdf: PNAS 2025, article e2306146122, published Feb 18 2025]*
   — Primary anchor for the hydrogen-reduction route: 24.3 ± 5.8 kWh/kg LOX,
   stage breakdown (reduction ~55%, water electrolysis ~38%), and the ~11.3
   kWh/kg O2 water-mining benchmark. The model's falsifiable validation target.

2. Kornuta, D., Abbud-Madrid, A., Atkinson, J., Barr, J., Barnhard, G., Bienhoff,
   D., et al. (2019). Commercial lunar propellant architecture: A collaborative
   study of lunar propellant production. *REACH — Reviews in Human Space
   Exploration*, **13**, 100026. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.reach.2019.100026
   *[verified — venue/volume/article confirmed via OSTI, ScienceDirect, Illinois Experts; PDF held as raw/clpa-kornuta-2019.pdf]*
   — CLPA: ~450 t/yr water-mining plant sized at ~2.0 MWe and the 2.8 MW
   plant figure; cross-check for the power-plant / landed-mass module.

3. Schreiner, S. S., Sibille, L., Dominguez, J. A., & Hoffman, J. A. (2016). A
   parametric sizing model for Molten Regolith Electrolysis reactors to produce
   oxygen on the Moon. *Advances in Space Research*, **57**(7), 1585–1603.
   https://doi.org/10.1016/j.asr.2016.01.029
   *[verified — authors, journal, volume/issue, pages confirmed via ScienceDirect/ResearchGate; DOI matches ScienceDirect PII S0273117716000296]*
   — MRE reactor sizing: whole-system kWh/kg O2 figures (~50–120) once Joule
   heating and duty cycle are included; upper band for the MRE route.

4. Sibille, L., & Dominguez, J. A. (2012). Joule-heated molten regolith
   electrolysis reactor concepts for oxygen and metals production on the Moon and
   Mars. NASA Technical Reports Server, Document ID 20120003037. (Also published
   as AIAA 2012-639, 50th AIAA Aerospace Sciences Meeting.)
   https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20120003037
   *[verified — NTRS ID, title, authors (Kennedy Space Center / QinetiQ N. America), Jan 2012 confirmed via NTRS]*
   — Distinction between near-thermodynamic decomposition voltage and the much
   larger Joule-heating voltages; underpins the MRE cell-voltage prior.

5. Allanore, A. (2015). Features and challenges of molten oxide electrolytes for
   metal extraction. *Journal of the Electrochemical Society*, **162**(1),
   E13–E22. https://doi.org/10.1149/2.0451501jes
   *[verified — author, journal, volume/issue, pages, DOI confirmed via IOPscience/MIT DSpace]*
   — Terrestrial molten-oxide electrolysis cell voltages and current efficiency;
   the ~3.7 MWh/t Fe (~9 kWh/kg O2) well-insulated lower bound for MRE.

6. Chen, G. Z., Fray, D. J., & Farthing, T. W. (2000). Direct electrochemical
   reduction of titanium dioxide to titanium in molten calcium chloride.
   *Nature*, **407**(6802), 361–364. https://doi.org/10.1038/35030069
   *[verified — authors, journal, volume, pages, DOI confirmed via Nature]*
   — Foundational FFC Cambridge process; basis for the molten-salt route
   chemistry and voltages.

7. Lomax, B. A., Conti, M., Khan, N., Bennett, N. S., Ganin, A. Y., & Symes, M. D.
   (2020). Proving the viability of an electrochemical process for the
   simultaneous extraction of oxygen and production of metal alloys from lunar
   regolith. *Planetary and Space Science*, **180**, 104748.
   https://doi.org/10.1016/j.pss.2019.104748
   *[verified — authors, journal, volume, article number, DOI confirmed via ScienceDirect/Heriot-Watt/CORE]*
   — Metalysis / FFC Cambridge applied to lunar regolith simulant (96% O removal
   at ~950 °C); molten-salt route O-recovery and operating temperature.

8. Colaprete, A., Schultz, P., Heldmann, J., Wooden, D., Shirley, M., Ennico, K.,
   et al. (2010). Detection of water in the LCROSS ejecta plume. *Science*,
   **330**(6003), 463–468. https://doi.org/10.1126/science.1186986
   *[verified — confirmed against raw/colaprete-2010-lcross-water-detection.pdf: Science 330(6003):463, DOI 10.1126/science.1186986]*
   — LCROSS PSR ice grade 5.6 ± 2.9 wt%; the water-route feedstock composition
   prior.

9. Carr, B. B. (1963). Recovery of water or oxygen by reduction of lunar rock.
   *AIAA Journal*, **1**(4), 921–924. https://doi.org/10.2514/3.1674
   *[verified — title, journal, volume/issue, pages, DOI confirmed via AIAA. NOTE: the ~26.4 kWh/kg O2 figure attributed to Carr is reported by secondary sources (Schreiner et al. 2016), not lifted verbatim from the original; treat the energy value as secondary.]*
   — Earliest lunar-MRE-style oxygen energy estimate; independent historical
   anchor for the MRE route (~26.4 kWh/kg O2).

10. Hauch, A., Küngas, R., Blennow, P., Hansen, A. B., Hansen, J. B., Mathiesen,
    B. V., & Mogensen, M. B. (2020). Recent advances in solid oxide cell
    technology for electrolysis. *Science*, **370**(6513), eaba6118.
    https://doi.org/10.1126/science.aba6118
    *[verified — authors, journal, volume/issue, article number, DOI confirmed via Science/PubMed]*
    — Solid oxide electrolysis cell (SOEC) system efficiency (~0.67); the
    water-electrolysis efficiency parameter, set independently of Leger.

11. Oleson, S., Packard, T., Turnbull, E., Gibson, M., Rao, D., Barth, C., Wilson,
    S., Schmitz, P., Colozza, A., Klefman, B., Tian, L., & Mason, L. (2022). A
    deployable 40 kWe lunar fission surface power concept. NASA Glenn Research
    Center (Compass Team); presented at Nuclear and Emerging Technologies for
    Space (NETS) 2022. NASA Technical Reports Server, Document ID 20220004670.
    https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20220004670
    *[verified — title and author list confirmed against the downloaded NTRS PDF; DOI was a placeholder "to be added by ANS" in the manuscript]*
    — Fission Surface Power 40 kWe concept and specific-mass basis (~225 kg/kWe;
    150 kg/kWe target) for the landed-mass module.

12. Schreiner, S. S., Dominguez, J. A., Sibille, L., & Hoffman, J. A. (2016).
    Thermophysical property models for lunar regolith. *Advances in Space
    Research*, **57**(8), 1209–1222. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.asr.2015.12.035
    *[unverified — best available: cited as a companion to ref. 3 in Leger's reference list (raw PDF); volume/issue/pages and DOI inferred from ScienceDirect indexing and not confirmed field-by-field]*
    — Regolith heat-capacity and melting models behind the heating-stage
    sub-model.

## Secondary / supporting sources

    overview and evaluation. In J. S. Lewis, M. S. Matthews, & M. L. Guerrieri
